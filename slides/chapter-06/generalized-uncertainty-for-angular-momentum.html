Generalized uncertainty for angular momentum
Covariance strengthens the uncertainty relation for angular components
14 / 15
Guided reading

The Robertson bound follows from the imaginary part of an inner product. The Schrödinger form also retains the real covariance, giving the strongest general two-observable inequality.

Centered operators
\[\delta A=A-\langle A\rangle,\qquad \delta B=B-\langle B\rangle\]

Their norms are the variances: \(\|\delta A|\psi\rangle\|^2=(\Delta A)^2\).

Schwarz inequality
\[\|\delta A\psi\|^2\|\delta B\psi\|^2\ge|\langle\delta A\psi|\delta B\psi\rangle|^2\]

Splitting the inner product into symmetric and antisymmetric parts produces covariance and commutator terms.

Generalized relation
\[(\Delta A)^2(\Delta B)^2\ge\frac14|\langle[A,B]\rangle|^2+\frac14|\langle\{\delta A,\delta B\}\rangle|^2\]
Angular application
\[\Delta L_x\Delta L_y\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}|\langle L_z\rangle|\]

For |l,m⟩, the right side is \(\hbar^2|m|/2\).

Why the simple bound may be loose

At m = 0 the commutator expectation vanishes, so the Robertson lower bound is zero even though both transverse variances are nonzero. The bound is state-dependent and need not equal the actual product.

Covariance meaning

The anticommutator term measures correlated fluctuations. It matters when deviations of two observables are statistically linked, and it completes the uncertainty information omitted by the commutator-only form.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Uncertainty is structural

The inequality follows from Hilbert-space geometry, not from experimental disturbance. Commutators determine an irreducible lower bound, while the state determines whether that bound is saturated.
