Quantization of l and m
Finite ladder endpoints force integer or half-integer quantum numbers
6 / 15
Guided reading

The z projection cannot increase without bound while L² remains fixed. Positivity of transverse squared angular momentum forces the ladder to terminate at upper and lower states.

Why endpoints exist
\[\langle L_x^2+L_y^2\rangle=a-b^2\ge0\]

For fixed a, repeatedly changing b by ħ would eventually violate this inequality unless the ladder stops.

Upper endpoint

At \(b_{max}\), \(L_+|a,b_{max}\rangle=0\). Taking the norm and using \(L_-L_+=L^2-L_z^2-\hbar L_z\) gives

\[a=b_{max}(b_{max}+\hbar)\]
Lower endpoint

At \(b_{min}\), \(L_-|a,b_{min}\rangle=0\), giving \(a=b_{min}(b_{min}-\hbar)\). Comparing endpoints yields \(b_{min}=-b_{max}\).

Counting equal steps

If n ladder steps connect \(-b_{max}\) to \(+b_{max}\), then \(2b_{max}=n\hbar\). Define \(l=n/2\), so l may be integer or half-integer.

\[l=0,\tfrac12,1,\tfrac32,\ldots\]
Defining m

Write \(b=m\hbar\). Since adjacent states differ by one ħ,

\[m=-l,-l+1,\ldots,l-1,l\]

The multiplet contains 2l+1 states.

Orbital versus spin

The abstract algebra permits half-integer l. Spatial orbital wave functions must be single-valued and yield integer l; intrinsic spin realizes half-integer representations. The distinction comes from representation, not from different commutators.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Quantization from consistency

The spectrum is forced by four ingredients: commutator step size, positivity, finite endpoints and symmetry of the ladder. No coordinate-space differential equation is needed.
