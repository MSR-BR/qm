Worked matrices and change of basis
Explicit low-dimensional matrices connect algebra with measurement bases
11 / 15
Guided reading

The l = 1 and l = 1/2 multiplets make the abstract rules concrete. Diagonalizing a transverse component reveals the state superpositions measured along a new axis.

Spin-one-half matrices
\[L_x=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix},\quad L_y=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0&-i\\i&0\end{pmatrix},\quad L_z=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix}\]
x eigenvectors
\[|+\rangle_x=\frac{|+\rangle_z+|-\rangle_z}{\sqrt2},\qquad |-\rangle_x=\frac{|+\rangle_z-|-\rangle_z}{\sqrt2}\]

A z-up state has equal probabilities for ±ħ/2 along x.

l = 1 matrices
\[L_z=\hbar\begin{pmatrix}1&0&0\\0&0&0\\0&0&-1\end{pmatrix},\qquad L_x=\frac{\hbar}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}0&1&0\\1&0&1\\0&1&0\end{pmatrix}\]
Change-of-basis matrix

Place normalized new-basis eigenvectors as columns of U. Unitarity, \(U^\dagger U=I\), preserves inner products. Depending on the convention for components, the transformed operator is \(U^\dagger AU\) or its equivalent inverse arrangement; state and operator conventions must match.

Diagonalization meaning

Diagonalizing Lx does not change the physical operator. It changes coordinates in Hilbert space so x-component eigenstates become basis vectors. Eigenvalues remain invariant, while matrix entries and state components change.

Measurement calculation

If \(|\psi\rangle\) is written in the z basis, the probability of x-up is \(|{}_x\langle+|\psi\rangle|^2\). This connects matrix diagonalization directly to Stern-Gerlach beam intensities.

Worked reasoning sequence

Use the following chain when working with Worked matrices and change of basis. First identify the symmetry, prepared state and observable being discussed; this determines which basis and quantum numbers are meaningful. Next write the operator or differential equation before substituting eigenvalues. Apply boundary conditions, commutators or normalization one step at a time, keeping dimensions and allowed quantum-number ranges visible. Only then simplify to the highlighted result. Finally translate the mathematics into a measurement statement: specify which outcomes are possible, which quantities remain uncertain, how degeneracy is counted, or where probability is concentrated. This sequence prevents a correct formula from becoming disconnected from the physical assumptions that justify it.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Basis independence

Probabilities and expectation values are invariant when both states and operators are transformed consistently. A basis change reorganizes the calculation, not the experiment.
