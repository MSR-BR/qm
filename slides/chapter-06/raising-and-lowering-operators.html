Raising and lowering operators
Ladder operators move between projections without changing magnitude
5 / 15
Guided reading

Combining Lx and Ly into non-Hermitian ladder operators converts the commutation algebra into a step-by-step construction of every state in one angular multiplet.

Definitions and inversion
\[\hat L_\pm=\hat L_x\pm i\hat L_y\]
\[\hat L_x=\frac{L_++L_-}{2},\qquad \hat L_y=\frac{L_+-L_-}{2i}\]
Central commutators
\[[\hat L_z,\hat L_\pm]=\pm\hbar\hat L_\pm\]
\[[\hat L^2,\hat L_\pm]=0\]

The first changes the z eigenvalue; the second preserves the L² eigenvalue.

Adjoints and products
\[\hat L_\pm^\dagger=\hat L_\mp\]
\[L_\mp L_\pm=L^2-L_z^2\mp\hbar L_z\]
Proving the step

Apply Lz to \(L_\pm|a,b\rangle\). Replace \(L_zL_\pm\) by \([L_z,L_\pm]+L_\pm L_z\). The result is \((b\pm\hbar)L_\pm|a,b\rangle\), so the new vector has a projection shifted by one ħ.

Preserving magnitude

Similarly, \(L^2L_\pm|a,b\rangle=L_\pm L^2|a,b\rangle=aL_\pm|a,b\rangle\). Raising changes orientation within a multiplet, not its total angular momentum.

Why they are not observables

L+ and L- are adjoints of each other rather than Hermitian individually, so they do not represent direct measurable quantities. They are construction operators whose combinations recover the observable transverse components.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Ladder logic

The commutators determine where a state goes; normalization determines with what coefficient it goes there. Endpoint states then determine the spectrum itself.
