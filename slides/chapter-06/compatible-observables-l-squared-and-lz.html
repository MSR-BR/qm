Compatible observables: L squared and Lz
Total magnitude and one projection define a complete angular basis
4 / 15
Guided reading

Although the components are mutually incompatible, the scalar operator L² commutes with every component. This allows magnitude and one chosen projection to be specified simultaneously.

Magnitude operator
\[\hat L^2=\hat L_x^2+\hat L_y^2+\hat L_z^2\]

It is rotationally invariant: changing axes does not change the total squared magnitude.

Commutator proof for z

Expand \([L_x^2+L_y^2+L_z^2,L_z]\). Using \([A^2,B]=A[A,B]+[A,B]A\), the x and y contributions cancel pairwise, while the z term is zero.

\[[\hat L^2,\hat L_z]=0\]
Common eigenvectors
\[\hat L^2|a,b\rangle=a|a,b\rangle,\qquad \hat L_z|a,b\rangle=b|a,b\rangle\]

The algebra will determine the initially unknown eigenvalues a and b.

Why choose z?

Any direction could be selected because L² commutes with every component. Choosing z is a convention that defines the quantization axis and simplifies matrices and spherical coordinates.

Compatible versus identical

Commutation allows simultaneous eigenvectors but does not make the observables the same. L² answers for total magnitude; Lz answers for one signed projection. Many m states share the same l magnitude.

Spectroscopic meaning

A rotationally invariant Hamiltonian can be diagonalized together with L² and Lz. Energy often depends on l but not m, producing a (2l+1)-fold orientation degeneracy that external fields may split.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

The basis choice controls the story

Once z is chosen, |l,m⟩ states are sharp in Lz and uncertain transversely. A different axis gives an equally valid basis related by a unitary rotation.
