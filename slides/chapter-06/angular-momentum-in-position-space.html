Angular momentum in position space
Orbital angular momentum is a purely angular differential operator
12 / 15
Guided reading

For spatial wave functions, momentum becomes -iħ∇. Substitution into r × p yields differential operators that generate infinitesimal rotations.

Vector operator
\[\hat{\mathbf L}=-i\hbar\,\mathbf r\times\nabla\]

Acting on a radial function alone gives zero because its gradient is parallel to r.

Cartesian components
\[L_x=-i\hbar\left(y\frac{\partial}{\partial z}-z\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\right)\]
\[L_z=-i\hbar\left(x\frac{\partial}{\partial y}-y\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\right)\]
Spherical z component
\[\hat L_z=-i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial\phi}\]

Acting on \(e^{im\phi}\) immediately gives \(m\hbar\).

L squared on the sphere
\[\hat L^2=-\hbar^2\left[\frac1{\sin\theta}\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\left(\sin\theta\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\right)+\frac1{\sin^2\theta}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial\phi^2}\right]\]
Purely angular character

No radial derivative appears in L² or its components. Therefore any radial multiplier commutes with orbital angular momentum. This is why central potentials V(r) are compatible with L² and Lz.

Generator interpretation

For a small rotation \(\delta\phi\) about z, the state changes by \(1-i\delta\phi L_z/\hbar\). The derivative with respect to azimuth is therefore the coordinate representation of the rotation generator.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Algebra meets coordinates

The differential operators obey the same commutators as the abstract operators. Coordinate representation changes how the operators act, not the angular-momentum algebra.
