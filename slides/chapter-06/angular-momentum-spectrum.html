Angular momentum spectrum
Magnitude, projection and multiplet dimension form the angular spectrum
7 / 15
Guided reading

Replacing the temporary endpoint eigenvalues by l and m produces the standard angular-momentum spectrum and a geometric picture with quantized projections.

Eigenvalue equations
\[\hat L^2|l,m\rangle=\hbar^2l(l+1)|l,m\rangle\]
\[\hat L_z|l,m\rangle=\hbar m|l,m\rangle\]
Allowed values
\[l=0,\tfrac12,1,\tfrac32,\ldots,\qquad m=-l,-l+1,\ldots,l\]

Fixing l defines one irreducible multiplet of dimension 2l+1.

Magnitude is not lħ
\[|\mathbf L|=\hbar\sqrt{l(l+1)}\]

The maximum known projection is \(l\hbar\), always smaller than the magnitude for l > 0. Some transverse spread must remain.

Vector-cone picture

A state |l,m⟩ fixes the magnitude and z projection. A semiclassical drawing places L on a cone with \(\cos\theta=m/\sqrt{l(l+1)}\). The azimuth is undetermined; the cone is a visualization of missing transverse information, not a literal trajectory.

Rotational degeneracy

If the Hamiltonian is rotationally invariant and energy depends on l, all 2l+1 m states have the same energy. Choosing a direction through a magnetic field typically breaks this degeneracy.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.

Geometry with quantum limits

The cone summarizes exact eigenvalues but should not be read as simultaneous values of Lx and Ly. Only their means and variances can be assigned in an Lz eigenstate.
