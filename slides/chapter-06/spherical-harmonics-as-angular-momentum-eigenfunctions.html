Spherical harmonics as angular momentum eigenfunctions
Spherical harmonics realize the abstract |l,m⟩ states on the sphere
13 / 15
Guided reading

The angular functions derived by separation of variables are precisely the position-space wave functions of simultaneous L² and Lz eigenstates.

Eigenvalue equations
\[L^2Y_l^m=\hbar^2l(l+1)Y_l^m\]
\[L_zY_l^m=\hbar mY_l^m\]
General form
\[Y_l^m(\theta,\phi)=N_{lm}P_l^m(\cos\theta)e^{im\phi}\]

Periodicity creates integer m; regularity at the poles restricts integer l and |m| ≤ l.

Ladder action on functions
\[L_\pm Y_l^m=\hbar\sqrt{l(l+1)-m(m\pm1)}Y_l^{m\pm1}\]

Starting from an endpoint harmonic, the entire multiplet can be constructed algebraically.

Orbital restriction

Abstract angular momentum admits integer and half-integer l. Ordinary scalar wave functions on the sphere yield only integer l because they must return to the same value after a 2π rotation. Spinors require a different representation.

Orthogonality
\[\int Y_{l'm'}^*(\Omega)Y_l^m(\Omega)d\Omega=\delta_{ll'}\delta_{mm'}\]

This makes spherical harmonics a natural basis for any angular wave function.

Physical probability

\(|Y_l^m|^2d\Omega\) is the angular probability in a solid-angle element. The phase \(e^{im\phi}\) controls Lz even when its modulus removes explicit azimuthal dependence from the density.

Algebra-to-physics audit

Before accepting an angular-momentum calculation, verify four layers. Operator layer: preserve order and use the correct commutator sign. Spectrum layer: confirm that m changes only in unit steps and remains between -l and +l. Representation layer: state the basis order before reading matrix rows or columns, and check Hermiticity for observables. Physics layer: distinguish an exact eigenvalue from an expectation value or variance. A zero transverse mean does not imply a zero transverse measurement; a basis rotation does not change the underlying observable; and a semiclassical vector cone does not assign simultaneous sharp values to all three components. These checks connect the formal algebra to what an actual sequence of measurements can reveal.
