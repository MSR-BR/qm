3D oscillator wave functions and probability
Angular momentum moves radial probability outward
12 / 15
Guided reading

The oscillator's total state combines a normalized Laguerre radial function with a spherical harmonic. Nodes and effective turning points organize how probability is distributed in three dimensions.

Normalized structure
\[\Psi_{Nlm}=R_{N l}(r)Y_l^m(\theta,\phi)\]

The radial polynomial degree is \(n_r=(N-l)/2\); normalization uses \(\int_0^\infty|R|^2r^2dr=1\).

Radial probability
\[P_{Nl}(r)=r^2|R_{Nl}(r)|^2\]

It vanishes at r = 0 because of the volume factor, even for an s state with finite R(0).

Effect of l

At fixed shell N, larger l strengthens the centrifugal term \(\hbar^2l(l+1)/(2mr^2)\), suppressing small-r probability and shifting the distribution outward.

Turning points

Classical radial turning points satisfy

\[E_N=\frac12m\omega^2r^2+\frac{\hbar^2l(l+1)}{2mr^2}\]

Quantum probability extends into the classically forbidden regions.

How l changes radial probability

At fixed N, increasing l forces n_r=(N-l)/2 to decrease. The wave function gains stronger small-r suppression \(r^l\) while losing radial nodes. The centrifugal contribution moves the inner turning point outward, so probability shifts away from the origin even though the total energy is unchanged. This is a clear example of degenerate states having different spatial distributions.

Interpreting m within one radial state

For fixed N and l, the radial function is identical for every m. Changing m alters only the spherical harmonic and thus the angular probability pattern. A measurement of r cannot distinguish m states, whereas a measurement of orientation or \(L_z\) can. This separation of radial and angular information is exactly what central symmetry permits.
