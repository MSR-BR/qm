Hydrogen radial equation and Laguerre solutions
Asymptotic factors leave an associated Laguerre polynomial
6 / 15
Guided reading

The hydrogen radial equation is solved by extracting the known small-r and large-r behavior. The remaining power series is normalizable only when it terminates, producing associated Laguerre polynomials.

Behavior near the origin

The regular radial solution behaves as \(R(r)\sim r^l\). The singular branch is rejected because the complete state must remain physically acceptable.

\[u(r)=rR(r)\sim r^{l+1}\]
Behavior at infinity

For a bound state, E is negative. Far from the nucleus the decaying solution is

\[R(r)\sim e^{-\kappa r},\qquad \kappa=\frac{\sqrt{-2mE}}{\hbar}\]
Dimensionless radial form

With \(\rho=r/a_0\) and the asymptotic factors removed, the remaining equation has polynomial solutions.

\[R_{nl}(r)=N_{nl}\,e^{-\rho/n}\left(\frac{2\rho}{n}\right)^l L_{n-l-1}^{2l+1}\!\left(\frac{2\rho}{n}\right)\]
Termination condition

The Laguerre series terminates only if

\[n-l-1=n_r=0,1,2,\ldots\]

Thus \(n=l+1+n_r\), where \(n_r\) counts radial nodes.

Special functions are structured boundary conditions

Laguerre polynomials are not decorative notation. Their finite degree is the mathematical record of normalizability and determines the allowed quantum numbers.
