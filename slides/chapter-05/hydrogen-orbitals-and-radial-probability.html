Hydrogen orbitals and radial probability
Radial density identifies nodes, shells and most probable distances
8 / 15
Guided reading

The total wave function is a product of a hydrogen radial function and a spherical harmonic. Position probability must include the spherical volume element, so radial probability is not simply |R|².

Complete orbital
\[\Psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\phi)=R_{nl}(r)Y_l^m(\theta,\phi)\]

The radial function fixes size and radial nodes; the spherical harmonic fixes angular nodes and directional structure.

Radial probability density
\[P_{nl}(r)=r^2|R_{nl}(r)|^2\]
\[\Pr(r\to r+dr)=P_{nl}(r)\,dr\]

The factor r² comes from the growth of spherical shell volume.

Ground-state check
\[R_{10}(r)=2a_0^{-3/2}e^{-r/a_0}\]
\[P_{10}(r)=4\frac{r^2}{a_0^3}e^{-2r/a_0}\]

Although \(|\Psi_{100}|^2\) is largest at the origin, \(P_{10}\) is largest at \(r=a_0\).

Density depends on the question

Use \(|\Psi|^2dV\) for a small volume near a point, but use \(r^2|R|^2dr\) for the probability of a distance from the nucleus. These are different measurements.
