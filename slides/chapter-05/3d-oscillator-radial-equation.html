3D oscillator radial equation
Regularity and Gaussian decay lead to generalized Laguerre functions
10 / 15
Guided reading

The radial 3D oscillator follows the same asymptotic logic used for hydrogen, but its large-r decay is Gaussian because the potential grows as r².

Boundary behavior

Near the origin, the regular branch is set by angular momentum; at infinity, confinement imposes Gaussian decay:

\[R(r)\sim r^l\quad(r\to0),\qquad R(r)\sim e^{-r^2/(2b^2)}\quad(r\to\infty)\]
Polynomial factor

With \(x=r^2/b^2\), write

\[R_{n_rl}(r)=N_{n_rl}\left(\frac{r}{b}\right)^l e^{-r^2/(2b^2)}L_{n_r}^{l+1/2}\!\left(\frac{r^2}{b^2}\right)\]
Radial quantum number

The nonnegative integer \(n_r\) is the degree of the Laguerre polynomial and equals the number of radial nodes.

\[n_r=0,1,2,\ldots\]
Shell relation

The Cartesian shell number and spherical labels obey

\[N=2n_r+l\]

Therefore l has the same parity as N and takes the values \(N,N-2,\ldots\).

Compare the two oscillators

The 1D oscillator produces Hermite polynomials in x; the radial 3D problem produces associated Laguerre polynomials in r². Both arise after extracting a Gaussian envelope.
