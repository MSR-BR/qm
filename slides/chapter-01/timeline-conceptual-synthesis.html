Timeline and conceptual synthesis
From empirical clues to wave mechanics

Guided reading

Use this page as the chapter's map. The timeline should be read from left to right as a causal chain: experiments expose a classical failure, a quantum rule repairs that failure, and the rule later becomes part of wave mechanics.

The central lesson is that old quantum physics finds the correct constraints before it has the correct language. Spectra, photons, matter waves and action quantization all point to the same destination: allowed states must be obtained from wave equations, boundary conditions and eigenvalues.

Historical flow

The chapter is a chain of constraints, not a loose list of discoveries. Each experiment removes one classical freedom and replaces it with a quantum condition.
