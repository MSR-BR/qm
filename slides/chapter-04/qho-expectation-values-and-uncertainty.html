QHO: expectation values and uncertainty
Moments of position and momentum reveal the minimum uncertainty scale
6 / 15
Guided reading

The ladder representation makes expectation values almost mechanical: express \(\hat x\) and \(\hat p\) in terms of \(\hat a\) and \(\hat a^\dagger\), then use orthogonality of number states. Parity predicts the vanishing odd moments before any calculation.

Invert the ladder definitions
\[\hat x=\frac{x_0}{\sqrt2}(\hat a+\hat a^\dagger),\qquad \hat p=\frac{\hbar}{i\sqrt2\,x_0}(\hat a-\hat a^\dagger)\]

Since \(\hat a|n\rangle\) and \(\hat a^\dagger|n\rangle\) are orthogonal to \(|n\rangle\),

\[\langle x\rangle_n=\langle p\rangle_n=0\]
Second moments
\[\langle x^2\rangle_n=x_0^2\left(n+\frac12\right)\]
\[\langle p^2\rangle_n=\frac{\hbar^2}{x_0^2}\left(n+\frac12\right)\]
Uncertainty product
\[\Delta x=x_0\sqrt{n+\frac12},\qquad \Delta p=\frac{\hbar}{x_0}\sqrt{n+\frac12}\]
\[\Delta x\,\Delta p=\hbar\left(n+\frac12\right)\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}\]

Only the ground state saturates the Heisenberg bound.

Energy partition

Using \(\hat T=\hat p^2/2m\) and \(V=m\omega^2x^2/2\),

\[\langle T\rangle_n=\langle V\rangle_n=\frac{E_n}{2}\]

This is the oscillator form of the virial theorem and provides a strong consistency check.

A fast reasoning route

Use symmetry first: odd moments vanish in parity eigenstates. Use ladder operators second: only terms that return \(|n\rangle\) to itself survive the inner product. This avoids lengthy Gaussian integrals.
