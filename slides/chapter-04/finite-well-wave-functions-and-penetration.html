Chapter 4 · Item 4.9
Finite-well wave functions and penetration
Finite confinement leaves observable probability beyond the classical boundaries
9 / 15
Guided reading

Once a root is known, the regional amplitudes are fixed by matching and one final normalization. The resulting eigenfunctions resemble infinite-well states inside, but their exponential tails alter energies, node locations and spatial probabilities.

Amplitude matching

For an even state, continuity at \(a\) gives

\[B=A\cos(ka)e^{\kappa a}\]

For an odd state, replace \(\cos(ka)\) by \(\sin(ka)\). A single overall constant remains and is determined from

\[2\int_0^a|\psi|^2dx+2\int_a^\infty|\psi|^2dx=1\]
Penetration probability

For a tail written as \(\psi(x)=\psi(a)e^{-\kappa(x-a)}\) for \(x\ge a\),

\[P_{x>a}=\int_a^\infty |\psi(a)|^2e^{-2\kappa(x-a)}dx=\frac{|\psi(a)|^2}{2\kappa}\]
Parity and nodes

The ground state is even and nodeless. Excited states alternate odd/even, acquiring one additional node each time. The probability density is always even for this symmetric potential.

\[\psi(-x)=\pm\psi(x),\qquad |\psi(-x)|^2=|\psi(x)|^2\]
Physical interpretation
  • Leakage is not a time-dependent escape from a stationary bound state.
  • It is a persistent probability of finding the particle where \(E<V\).
  • States near threshold have the strongest leakage.
  • The infinite-well limit is recovered as \(V_0\to\infty\), when \(\kappa\to\infty\).
Normalization includes both tails

For an even state, the total norm is twice the integral over \(x\ge0\). The interior contribution contains \(\int_0^a\cos^2kx\,dx=a/2+\sin(2ka)/(4k)\). The exterior contributes \(|B|^2/(2\kappa)\). Using \(B=A\cos ka\) leaves one equation for A. Neglecting the tail would overestimate the interior amplitude and give incorrect probabilities.

Energy controls leakage

The penetration length is \(1/\kappa=\hbar/\sqrt{-2mE}\). A state close to the continuum threshold has small \(|E|\), small \(\kappa\), and a long tail. A deeply bound state has a short tail. Increasing mass also reduces penetration. These trends can be predicted before calculating the normalization and are useful for checking a plotted eigenfunction.

Wave function first, probability second

A plotted wave function shows phase, sign and nodes; a probability density shows measurable position statistics. Do not interpret negative lobes of \(\psi\) as negative probability.
