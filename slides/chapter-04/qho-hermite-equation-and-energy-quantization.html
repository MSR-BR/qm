QHO: Hermite equation and energy quantization
Normalizability turns a differential equation into a discrete spectrum
3 / 15
Guided reading

After the Gaussian asymptotic factor is removed, the remaining function satisfies Hermite's equation. A generic power series grows too rapidly; normalizability forces that series to terminate, and this termination condition quantizes the energy.

Factor out the Gaussian

Write

\[\psi(\xi)=h(\xi)e^{-\xi^2/2}\]

and substitute into the dimensionless equation. The result is

\[h''-2\xi h'+(2\epsilon-1)h=0\]

This becomes the Hermite equation only for particular values of \(\epsilon\).

Series recursion

With \(h=\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}a_j\xi^j\), coefficients satisfy

\[a_{j+2}=\frac{2j+1-2\epsilon}{(j+2)(j+1)}a_j\]

Even and odd coefficients form independent chains, anticipating states of definite parity.

Termination and quantization

The series must terminate at some nonnegative integer \(n\). Setting the numerator to zero at \(j=n\) gives

\[2n+1-2\epsilon=0\]
\[E_n=\hbar\omega\left(n+\frac12\right),\qquad n=0,1,2,\ldots\]
What is actually quantized?
  • The energy levels are equally spaced: \(E_{n+1}-E_n=\hbar\omega\).
  • The ground state has nonzero energy \(E_0=\hbar\omega/2\).
  • The integer \(n\) is both the polynomial degree and the number of nodes.
  • Quantization follows from normalizability, not from an imposed wall.
Why the series must stop

For large index \(j\), the recursion approaches \(a_{j+2}\approx2a_j/j\). The resulting series grows approximately as \(e^{\xi^2}\). Multiplying by the extracted Gaussian \(e^{-\xi^2/2}\) would therefore leave a divergent \(e^{+\xi^2/2}\), which cannot be normalized. The only escape is for one numerator in the recursion to vanish, terminating either the even or the odd coefficient chain. That single algebraic event creates an acceptable polynomial.

From termination to equally spaced energy

If the highest power is \(\xi^n\), the recursion numerator vanishes when \(2\epsilon-1=2n\). Thus \(\epsilon=n+1/2\), and restoring units gives \(E_n=\hbar\omega(n+1/2)\). The spacing is constant because the dimensionless equation has no other energy scale. The half quantum remains even at \(n=0\); removing it would contradict both the differential equation and the uncertainty principle.

The logical hinge

The differential equation allows many formal series, but the Hilbert-space requirement \(\int|\psi|^2dx<\infty\) rejects almost all of them. Physical acceptability is the boundary condition that creates the spectrum.
