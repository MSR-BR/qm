QHO: ladder operators and oscillator algebra
Hamiltonian factorization constructs the spectrum without solving a differential equation
5 / 15
Guided reading

The ladder-operator method exposes the algebra behind the QHO spectrum. Position and momentum combine into operators that raise or lower the excitation number by one quantum while preserving the equally spaced structure.

Define the operators
\[\hat a=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\left(\frac{\hat x}{x_0}+i\frac{x_0\hat p}{\hbar}\right),\qquad \hat a^\dagger=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\left(\frac{\hat x}{x_0}-i\frac{x_0\hat p}{\hbar}\right)\]
\[[\hat a,\hat a^\dagger]=1\]
Factor the Hamiltonian

With the number operator \(\hat N=\hat a^\dagger\hat a\),

\[\hat H=\hbar\omega\left(\hat N+\frac12\right)\]

Because \(\langle\psi|\hat N|\psi\rangle=\lVert\hat a|\psi\rangle\rVert^2\ge0\), its eigenvalues cannot be negative.

Ladder action
\[\hat a|n\rangle=\sqrt n\,|n-1\rangle,\qquad \hat a^\dagger|n\rangle=\sqrt{n+1}\,|n+1\rangle\]

The commutators \([\hat H,\hat a]=-\hbar\omega\hat a\) and \([\hat H,\hat a^\dagger]=+\hbar\omega\hat a^\dagger\) show that one application changes the energy by exactly one quantum.

Build every state

The lowering chain must stop at a ground state satisfying

\[\hat a|0\rangle=0\]

Solving this first-order equation produces the Gaussian. Higher states follow algebraically:

\[|n\rangle=\frac{(\hat a^\dagger)^n}{\sqrt{n!}}|0\rangle\]
Algebra and wave functions agree

The polynomial generated by repeated \(\hat a^\dagger\) is exactly the Hermite polynomial found by the series method. The two approaches are different routes to the same eigenstates and spectrum.
