Guided reading

The chapter's apparent variety reduces to a small set of decisions. First classify the energy regime. Then select the local mathematical behavior, enforce the correct joining rule, and use either normalization or current conservation to extract physics.

The universal decision tree
QuestionConsequence
Is \(E>V(x)\)?Oscillatory local solution
Is \(E<V(x)\)?Exponential local solution
Must the state be localized?Discard growth; normalize
Is the potential symmetric?Choose even or odd parity
Is the state scattering?Use incident/reflected/transmitted current
Is the potential singular?Derive the matching jump by integration
Three origins of discrete structure
  • QHO: series termination forced by normalizability.
  • Finite well: transcendental boundary matching.
  • Resonant scattering: phase matching creates discrete transparency energies.

Conceptual synthesis

Classically forbidden does not mean quantum mechanically absent: bound states leak and scattering states tunnel. Discrete energies do not require literal rigid walls: they arise whenever global acceptability restricts a continuum of formal local solutions.

Probability is conserved throughout. For bound states this appears as normalization; for scattering states it appears as conserved current.

One chapter, one habit

Do not begin by searching for a memorized formula. Sketch the potential, compare \(E\) with \(V\) in every region, write the allowed local forms, and let boundary conditions determine the rest.
