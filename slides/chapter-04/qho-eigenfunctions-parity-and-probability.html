QHO: eigenfunctions, parity and probability
Hermite polynomials encode nodes, symmetry and measurable position statistics
4 / 15
Guided reading

The QHO eigenfunctions combine a universal Gaussian envelope with a Hermite polynomial. Their parity alternates with \(n\), their number of nodes equals \(n\), and their probability densities spread farther from equilibrium as the energy rises.

Normalized eigenfunctions
\[\psi_n(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n n!\,\sqrt{\pi}\,x_0}}\,H_n\!\left(\frac{x}{x_0}\right)e^{-x^2/(2x_0^2)}\]
\(n\)\(H_n(\xi)\)Parity
01even
1\(2\xi\)odd
2\(4\xi^2-2\)even
3\(8\xi^3-12\xi\)odd
Nodes and orthogonality

A degree-\(n\) Hermite polynomial has \(n\) real zeros, so \(\psi_n\) has \(n\) nodes. Distinct stationary states satisfy

\[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\psi_m^*(x)\psi_n(x)\,dx=\delta_{mn}\]
Probability and parity

Because \(|\psi_n|^2\) is even for every \(n\), symmetric intervals carry equal probability. The wave function itself obeys

\[\psi_n(-x)=(-1)^n\psi_n(x)\]

Therefore \(\langle x\rangle=0\) in every energy eigenstate.

Classical turning points

The points where \(E_n=V(x)\) are

\[x_t=\pm\sqrt{\frac{2E_n}{m\omega^2}}=\pm x_0\sqrt{2n+1}\]

Quantum probability extends beyond these points. For large \(n\), the rapidly oscillating quantum distribution approaches the coarse-grained classical residence-time distribution.

How to read an oscillator plot

The sign of \(\psi_n\) matters for interference and orthogonality, but a position measurement samples \(|\psi_n|^2\). Each zero of the Hermite polynomial becomes a node of both quantities. The parity alternates because \(H_n(-\xi)=(-1)^nH_n(\xi)\). Therefore even n states are even and odd n states are odd; in either case the density is symmetric and all odd position moments vanish.

Quantum probability beyond the turning point

At \(|x|=x_t\), the classical kinetic energy reaches zero. Beyond it, the stationary Schrödinger equation produces an exponential tail rather than an abrupt cutoff. This does not imply a classical trajectory with negative kinetic energy. It means position and kinetic energy are not simultaneously sharp. As n grows, the turning points move outward and rapid quantum oscillations average toward the classical distribution, illustrating correspondence without eliminating nodes.

Zero-point motion

The ground state cannot collapse to \(x=0\). Extreme localization would make the momentum uncertainty—and hence the kinetic energy—diverge. The Gaussian ground state balances kinetic and potential contributions at \(E_0=\hbar\omega/2\).
